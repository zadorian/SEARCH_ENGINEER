== Corporate Registry ==

=== Companies in Japan ===
Japanese companies are categorized under various types including Kabushiki Kaisha (KK) and Godo Kaisha (GK).

Kabushiki Kaisha (KK) is a type of corporation similar to a public company in the Western context, where shares can be offered to the public. Godo Kaisha (GK) is similar to a limited liability company, typically used for smaller businesses.

See the detailed regulations for each type of company in Japan Regulatory.pdf

=== Japanese Corporate Register ===
[Japanese Corporate Register](http://www1.touki.or.jp/gateway.html)
The Japanese Corporate Register is maintained by the Ministry of Justice and provides comprehensive information about companies registered in Japan. Access to the database requires a login.

=== Japanese Corporate Register - Information ===
[Japanese Corporate Register - Information](http://www1.touki.or.jp)
This site run by the Ministry of Justice allows searching for companies, directors, and ownership information. Details available include company name, registration number, and directors' information.

=== Japanese Corporate Register - Business Names ===
[Japanese Corporate Register - Business Names](http://www1.touki.or.jp)
You can search the business names register by the family and given name of a person holding business names. The information retrieved includes current person name details, roles held, and current business name details.

=== Japanese Corporate Register - Public View ===
[Japanese Corporate Register - Public View](http://www1.touki.or.jp)
Provides access to publicly available information supplied by businesses when they register. Supports search by company's name and registration number.

=== Ownership ===
Ownership details are generally unclear and require specific searches through the corporate register. Reverse searches are not commonly available.

=== Directorship ===
Directorship information can be obtained through the Japanese Corporate Register. Details include the directors' names and roles within the company.

== Litigation ==

=== Court System in Japan ===
Japan has a well-structured court system divided into several levels including the Supreme Court, High Courts, District Courts, Family Courts, and Summary Courts. Each level of court handles different types of cases ranging from criminal, civil, to family matters.

=== Key Resources ===

==== AustLII ====
[AustLII](http://www6.austlii.edu.au/)
This database contains full text decisions of various Japanese courts and publishes public legal information including primary legal materials (legislation, treaties, and court decisions).

==== JADE ====
[JADE](https://jade.io/)
The Judgment and Decisions Enhanced website provides a professional legal research platform designed for the legal profession. It includes decisions of Japanese and international courts and tribunals.

== Political ==

=== Political System ===
Japan is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary government. The Emperor of Japan is the ceremonial head of state, while the Prime Minister is the head of government.
