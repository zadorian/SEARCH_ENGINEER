Denmark has a civil law system with a judicial review of legislative acts. Additionally it accepts compulsory International Court of Justice jurisdiction with reservations. The highest Danish court is the Supreme Court, consisting out of 18 judges and the court president. The Danish supreme courts accepts cases from the Maritime and Commercial Court and the High Court (Court of Appeals). In turn, the High Court accepts cases from the county courts. Furthermore, the Danish Constitution provides for the Court of Impeachment of the Realm (Rigsretten) to hear cases brought against ministers concerning their administration. In addition, the Special Court of Final Appeal (Den særlige Klageret) deals with cases concerning disciplinary sanctions against judges and petitions for retrial of criminal cases under Article 86 of the Administration of Justice Act.

Court Structure:

- Supreme Court

- High Court

- Maritime and Commercial Court

- Special Court of Indictment and Revision

- Land Registration Court

- Greenland Courts

- Faroe Island Courts

- County Court
