==Litigation==

===Disclaimer===

We searched litigation records in Florida, including reported and unreported cases heard by the Supreme Court, District Courts of Appeal, Circuit Courts, and County Courts. Not all litigation records are publicly available online. Records from certain counties may require access via third-party services such as EZyCheck. Therefore, these searches should not be considered comprehensive.

===Legal System===

The Florida trial court system consists of Circuit Courts, County Courts, and Miami-Dade County Courts. Florida has 93 courts, including a supreme court, five appellate courts, 20 circuit courts, and 67 county courts. The Circuit Courts hear property disputes over USD 15,000, mental health cases, probate disputes, domestic relations, juvenile matters, and felony cases. The County Courts hear property cases under USD 15,000, small claims, traffic, and misdemeanor cases. In Miami-Dade County, the jurisdiction of Circuit Court and County Court is combined and organized into several divisions, including Civil Court, Criminal Court, Family Court, Juvenile Court and Traffic Court.
