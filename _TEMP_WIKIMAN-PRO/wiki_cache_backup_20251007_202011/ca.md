==Canada==

==Corporate Registry==

===National Corporate Records===

[https://www.ic.gc.ca/eic/site/cd-dgc.nsf/eng/home Corporations Canada] (''pub'')

Corporations Canada offers a registry for entities incorporated at the federal level. Researchers can search by company name or registration number to access information such as:
- Company name
- Registered office address
- Names and addresses of directors
- Incorporation date
- Corporate status

Note: The data here might be periodically scraped by third-party aggregators like OpenCorporates, but it's essential to verify information against the official source for accuracy.

[https://beta.canadasbusinessregistries.ca/search Canada's Business Registries] (''pub'')

This service allows you to search across multiple provincial registries simultaneously, including Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, Ontario, and Quebec. Researchers can search by company name, business number, or registry ID.

Data Available:
- Entity type
- Directors and officers (current and former)
- Incorporation date
- Registered address

===Provincial Corporate Records===

[https://www.alberta.ca/business-search.aspx Alberta Business Search] (''pub'')

Alberta's corporate registration information can be accessed here. This site is useful for identifying a company's registered name and verifying its corporate status.

Data Available:
- Company name
- Registration details
- Names of directors (including home addresses)
- Corporate status

[https://www.bconline.gov.bc.ca/main.html British Columbia Registry Services] (''pub'', ''paid'', ''reg'')

BC Online provides access to corporate registration, civil, criminal, and property records. The service requires a login and offers comprehensive details.

[https://orgbook.gov.bc.ca/ OrgBook BC] (''pub'')

Use this database to verify if an organization is legally registered to do business in British Columbia as a corporation. You can also find "Doing Business As" names registered by corporations.

[https://www.gov.mb.ca/chc/archives/ Manitoba Business Information Service] (''pub'')

Call the service to access full corporate registration information for companies in Manitoba. The service provides shareholding structure and a list of directors and officers, including their home addresses.

[https://www.pxw1.snb.ca/snb7001/e/2000/2400e_1.asp Corporate Affairs Registry Database - New Brunswick] (''pub'')

This database allows you to search by keyword for corporate records in New Brunswick. Records include a list of directors and their home addresses.

[https://cado.eservices.gov.nl.ca/CADOInternet/Registry/Welcome.aspx Newfoundland Registry of Companies] (''pub'')

Search the CADO system for corporate records in Newfoundland. This service includes a list of directors and officers, along with their home addresses.

[https://www.iti.gov.nt.ca/en/services/business-services-directory NWT Corporate Registries Online System] (''pub'')

Run a basic name search as a guest to obtain more detailed information. The Northwest Territories registry provides additional information on nationally registered corporations.

[https://novascotia.ca/sns/access/business/registry-joint-stock-companies.asp Registry of Joint Stock Companies - Nova Scotia] (''pub'')

Search this database for business or society information on file with the Nova Scotia Registry of Joint Stock Companies. Records include company officers, activity history, and other related registrations.

[https://gov.nu.ca/edt/information/nunavut-corporate-registry Nunavut Corporate Registry] (''pub'')

Search by business name to access records that provide a "point of contact," usually an officer or director.

[https://www.ontario.ca/page/serviceontario ServiceOntario] (''pub'')

This service is used to identify companies registered in Ontario. Records include directors, officers, and an address, which may be residential.

[https://cyberbahn.ca/en/ Cyberbahn] (''pub'', ''paid'')

Use this service to search for Ontario corporate registrations if you can't otherwise identify directors and their home addresses. This service may involve a fee.

[https://www.princeedwardisland.ca/en/feature/pei-business-corporate-registry-original#/ Prince Edward Island Government Corporate Registry] (''pub'')

Search this database for corporate records in Prince Edward Island. Records include a list of directors and officers.

[https://www.registreentreprises.gouv.qc.ca/RQAnonymeGR/GR/GR03/GR03A2_19A_PIU_RechEnt_PC/PageRechSimple.aspx?T1.CodeService=S00436&amp;Clng=F&amp;WT.co_f=24a032c4ade39a97aad1557277350006 Revenu Quebec Business Name Search] (''pub'')

This service provides information on directors and executives in Quebec. It is advisable to cross-check the data against the official source for accuracy.

[https://www.ccmm.ca/en Chamber of Commerce of Metropolitan Montreal Member Directory] (''pub'')

Search this directory for companies that have partnered with the Chamber of Commerce of Metropolitan Montreal.

[https://nuans.com/en/home NUANS] (''pub'')

Run a search on this service to identify where a company is registered. It is not considered an authority for corporate registration sections in reports.

==Individual Identification==

Public information in Canada is limited due to strict privacy laws. Below are resources for individual identification.

[https://www.ic.gc.ca/eic/site/icgc.nsf/eng/home Innovation Science and Economic Development Canada] (''pub'')

Use the search function to find information related to business entities and intellectual property.

[https://www.sedar.com/homepage_en.htm SEDAR] (''pub'')

System for Electronic Document Analysis and Retrieval (SEDAR) is Canada's equivalent to EDGAR. It is essential for public companies/private placements.

[https://www.sedi.ca/sedi/SVD_SED/SEI.do?locale=en_CA SEDI] (''pub'')

The System for Electronic Disclosure by Insiders (SEDI) is crucial for verifying corporate affiliations and employment dates.

==Litigation==

[https://www.canlii.org/en/ Canadian Legal Information Institute (CanLII)] (''pub'')

The main online litigation source in Canada, CanLII aggregates Canadian regulatory and litigation information federally and by province. Boolean operators are allowed in searches.

[https://www.fct-cf.gc.ca/en/court-files-and-decisions/court-files Federal Court / Court of Appeal] (''pub'')

Search in the parties field or the full text search field for decisions from the Federal Court of Canada.

[https://www.bconline.gov.bc.ca/ BC Online Court Services] (''pub'', ''reg'')

This service allows you to search for BC court documents, including civil, criminal, and traffic cases.

[https://www.ontario.ca/page/serviceontario Ontario Justice Services Online] (''pub'')

Search for both civil and criminal case types in Ontario. Case number is not required for a search.

[https://www.courts.gov.bc.ca/ Provincial Court of British Columbia - Court Decisions] (''pub'')

Search for court decisions from the Provincial Court of British Columbia, including civil, criminal, and family law cases.

[https://www.bccourts.ca/supreme_court/index.aspx The Courts of British Columbia - Judgments Search] (''pub'')

This link provides access to judgments from the Supreme Court of British Columbia and the Court of Appeal for British Columbia.

==Education Verification==

[http://www.auradata.com/ Auradata] (''reg'')

A primary resource for online degree verification. Requires a signed release.

[https://www.ryerson.ca/ Ryerson University Degree Verification] (''pub'')

No signed release form required for degree verification at Ryerson University. Verification for degrees before 1984 requires a manual request.

[https://www.ucalgary.ca/ University of Calgary Degree Verification] (''pub'')

Call the registrar for degree verification without a signed release.

[https://www.utoronto.ca/ University of Toronto Degree Verification] (''pub'')

A signed release is not required for verification of degrees, diplomas, or certificates.

==Political==

[https://lobbycanada.gc.ca/app/secure/ocl/lrs/do/advSrch?lang=eng Office of the Commissioner of Lobbying in Canada] (''pub'')

Conduct a keyword search to identify lobbying activities.

[http://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/Eng/fi-if/amlc-clrpc/atf-fat/Pages/default.aspx OSFI Designated Entities: Terrorism Financing] (''pub'')

The Canadian Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) publishes a list of entities that are sanctioned because of their connections to terrorist financing under the Canadian Criminal Code and/or the Regulations Implementing the United Nations Resolutions on the Suppression of Terrorism and/or United Nations Al-Qaida and Taliban Regulations.

[http://www.publicsafety.gc.ca/cnt/ntnl-scrt/cntr-trrrsm/lstd-ntts/crrnt-lstd-ntts-en.aspx Canada Public Safety - Currently Listed Terrorist Entities] (''pub'')

A list of entities currently designated as terrorist entities under the Criminal Code by Public Safety Canada.

[http://www.international.gc.ca/sanctions/assests-freeze_list-liste_gel.aspx Foreign Affairs and International Trade Canada Sanctions] (''pub'')

The Department of Foreign Affairs and International Trade Canada maintains a list of assets frozen under Canadian sanctions regulations.

[http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/PDF/SOR-2007-285.pdf Special Economic Measures (Burma) Regulations] (''pub'')

A document consolidating special economic measures imposed on Burma, available through the Department of Justice.

[https://www.elections.ca/ Elections Canada] (''pub'')

Search for individuals' political contributions across all parties and political entities.
