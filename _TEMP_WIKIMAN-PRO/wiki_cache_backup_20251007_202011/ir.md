==Corporate Registry== 

* [https://search.cro.ie/company/CompanySearch.aspx Companies Registration Office] 
Official registry. Basic background data is provided on entities through a search, but the only way to get further information is to pay for individual corporate filings, which give only a basic description of changes. 

* [https://www.solocheck.ie/ SoloCheck] - a paid-for company check service which is more expensive, but much more efficient, than the CRO. 

A basic report (EUR 4) gives background data and company director names. A full credit report (EUR 10) identifies directors (with their DOBs, address and possible other affiliations based on name and DOB combination) and shareholders, alongside giving key financials for the past five years. A list of judgements may be provided if relevant. The latter option is the best - you are highly likely to spend at least EUR 10 trying to identify directors and shareholders by buying individual filings from the CRO (EUR 2.50 each). However, you should also check:

* [https://ie.globaldatabase.com/ Global Database] - an aggregator which has proven to identify Irish company shareholders for free in the past ([https://ie.globaldatabase.com/company/dave-barry-plastics-limited example]).

* [https://id.occrp.org/databases/#ireland OCCRP ID - Search government] gazettes for company-related notices (''pub'')

* [https://e-justice.europa.eu/content_business_registers_in_member_states-106-ie-en.do?member=1 European Commission E-Justice - Overview] of Irish business registry (''pub'')

* [https://register.rbo.gov.ie/ Registered Beneficial Owner (RBO) registry]  (''pub'', ''reg''). Not publicly accessible since 2022.

* [http://www.irisoifigiuil.ie/ Irish State Gazette - Official] company notices (''pub''). '''Note:''' not searchable

* [https://www.revenue.ie/ Irish Tax and Customs] The agency publishes a quarterly list of Revenue Defaulters. While press releases announcing the publication of such lists are available going back to 2020, as of May 2025, the relevant [https://www.revenue.ie/en/corporate/press-office/list-of-defaulters/index.aspx page] on the authority's website only showed results for 2023 and 2024. * '''Insolvency and bankruptcy'''
The [https://www.gov.ie/en/insolvency-service-of-ireland/services/insolvency-service-of-ireland-registers/#access-the-registers Insolvency Service] maintains a register presenting all Personal Insolvency Arrangements, Debt Relief Notices, and Debt Settlement Arrangements and Protective Certificates going back three months. Additionally, the [https://www.csol.ie/ccms/bankruptcy.html#/search Courts Service] also provides a bankruptcy search for individuals. 

For companies, the CRO registry [https://cro.ie/publications/cro-gazette/gazette-publications/ publishes] weekly lists of companies which have made corporate filings related to insolvency or winding-up. The CRO site is responsive to site search to identify such entities, but details are only available by buying said filings. Similarly, aggregator [https://www.businessbarometer.ie/company-insolvency-list Business Barometer] maintains a combined list of companies with recent wind-up filings. A subject company's page in the aggregator database should have any such filings highlighted in yellow.
