== Corporate Registry ==

=== Companies in Iceland ===
Icelandic companies are registered and maintained by the Ríkisskattstjóri (Directorate of Internal Revenue).

[Iceland Corporate Register](https://www.rsk.is/fyrirtaekjaskra)  
The Iceland companies register is run by the Ríkisskattstjóri (Directorate of Internal Revenue). It provides comprehensive information about companies registered in Iceland. The registry supports searches by company name and provides details such as company status, registration number, and more. Search by company name to get basic company information with information on postal address, municipality, legal form, type of business. Annual accounts are available for free download by email. (pub)

[Legal Gazette Iceland Lögbirtingablað](http://www.logbirtingablad.is/) (pub)

=== Ownership ===
Ownership information is available for companies listed in the registry. Reverse ownership searches are generally not available.

=== Directorship ===
Directorship information can be obtained through the Iceland Corporate Register. This includes details about the directors and their roles within the company.

== Litigation ==

=== Court System in Iceland ===
Iceland has a structured court system with various levels including District Courts and the Supreme Court. The District Courts handle both civil and criminal cases at the first instance, while the Supreme Court handles appeals.

[District Courts](https://www.heradsdomstolar.is/heradsdomstolar/)  
The Icelandic justice portal enables search for legal decisions and case information from the district courts. It provides access to court decisions, procedural history, and other relevant case details. This covers the seven district courts. (pub)

[Supreme Court](https://www.haestirettur.is/)  
The Supreme Court is responsible for appeals from the district courts. It provides access to final decisions on significant cases and maintains a searchable database of its rulings. The Supreme Court offers a search service for case law since 1999 on its website. However, parties to cases are anonymised. (pub)

== Regulatory ==

=== Financial Supervisory Authority ===
[Financial Supervisory Authority](https://www.fme.is)  
The Financial Supervisory Authority of Iceland oversees the financial sector, including banking, insurance, securities markets, and pension funds. It ensures compliance with laws and regulations and maintains financial stability. (pub)

=== Enforcement Actions and Noteworthy Cases ===
Information about enforcement actions and noteworthy cases is generally available through the respective regulatory bodies.

== Asset Registries ==

[European Patent Organisation](https://register.epo.org/advancedSearch)  
Iceland is a member of the European Patent Organisation. Detailed information about patents and applications can be found through the European Patent Register. This includes application status, legal status, and other patent-related information. Detailed information available on click through. (pub)

[Icelandic Patent Office Ministry of Economic Affairs](https://gamli.els.is/en) (pub)

== Political ==

=== Political System ===
Iceland is a parliamentary republic with a President as the head of state and a Prime Minister as the head of government. The Althing is Iceland's national parliament.

== Media ==
Iceland has a dynamic media landscape with several newspapers, television networks, and online platforms providing comprehensive news coverage.

=== Key Publications ===
- **Morgunblaðið**: One of Iceland's leading newspapers
- **RÚV**: Iceland's national public broadcasting organization
- **Fréttablaðið**: A widely circulated daily newspaper
