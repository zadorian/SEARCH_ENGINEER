==Litigation==

[https://www.ustavnisud.ba/ Constitutional Court of Bosnia and Herzegovina -] Handles constitutional disputes and human rights issues. Selected decisions are published online. (''pub'')

The Bosnian Court System includes multiple levels, from municipal to state-level courts. Public records, including civil, criminal, and administrative cases, can be accessed via the court's public information offices.

==Regulatory==

[https://www.uino.gov.ba/ Indirect Taxation Authority of Bosnia and Herzegovina -] This authority is responsible for overseeing indirect taxes, including VAT, customs, and excise duties. Public records related to tax enforcement actions can be accessed here. (''pub'')

[http://www.sluzbenilist.ba/ Official Gazette of Bosnia and Herzegovina -] The Official Gazette publishes laws, regulations, and official announcements. It serves as a public record for government actions. (''pub'')

[https://www.poreskaupravars.org/ Tax Administration of the Republic of Srpska -] This body manages tax collection and enforcement in the Republic of Srpska, one of the two entities of Bosnia and Herzegovina. Public records related to tax matters can be accessed through this administration. (''pub'')

[https://www.bihkonk.gov.ba/ The Council of Competition of Bosnia and Herzegovina -] The Council is responsible for maintaining fair competition in the market. It oversees mergers, monopolies, and antitrust issues. Public records related to competition law enforcement can be accessed here. (''pub'')

[http://www.ohr.int/ The Office of the High Representative -] This international body oversees the implementation of the civilian aspects of the peace agreement that ended the Bosnian War. While not a typical regulatory body, it may have records relevant to governance and legal matters in Bosnia and Herzegovina. (''pub'')

The Cadastral Registry maintains records of land ownership and property rights across Bosnia and Herzegovina. Information is available through the local municipal offices, and records can be accessed for a fee.

==Disclaimers==

Public records in Bosnia and Herzegovina may not always be up to date or complete due to the decentralized nature of record-keeping and administrative delays. It is advisable to verify the information from multiple sources when conducting research or due diligence.

While some public records are freely accessible, others may require a fee or formal request. Access to detailed financial or ownership information often requires authorization or proof of legitimate interest.

Bosnian law includes protections for personal data, and some records may be anonymized or restricted from public access to protect individual privacy rights. Requests for personal or sensitive information may require justification or legal permission.
